7. Cookies, network beacons and tracking

Our services may use cookies and pixel tags, local shared objects, clear GIFs and web beacons. We treat the information collected by cookies and similar technologies as non-personal information. Cookies: "cookies" are a small amount of recorded information often stored on users' computers by websites. Our cookies do not include personal information, and are generally used to quickly identify your device and "remember" you. You can cancel cookies, or set your browser to the mode that will prompt you when sending cookies to you. However, canceling cookies may affect your use of our services. Flash cookies and HTML5: We may use Flash cookies (locally shared objects) and HTML5 (locally stored objects). Local shared objects are small files similar to browser cookies, which are used to remember your settings and thus personalize the appearance of our services. Local shared objects only collect data in a consolidated way. You can prevent the installation of local shared objects by canceling local storage objects in your browser or visiting www.adobe.com. Local storage objects such as HTML5 Web Storage are similar to cookies, but generally contain more information than browser cookies. Network beacon and pixel label: "Network beacon" or "pixel label" is an electronic image that allows us to calculate the number of page visits or advertising views. Similar to cookies, network beacons do not contain personal information. The email and other electronic communications we send to you may contain pixel tags or network tags, enabling us to track the use of communications, such as whether the communications have been opened, which links have been clicked, etc.
